The scatter plot with regression line shows that as unemployment rises, median weekly sales decline gradually. However, the data points are widely dispersed, which suggests:

  • Spending stickiness: As a major retailer, many Walmart products are necessities and are relatively less sensitive to economic swings.
  • Outlier analysis: Some stores maintain very high sales even in high-unemployment areas, possibly due to limited local competition or larger store formats.

Comparing long-term trends for selected stores (Store 1, 4, 20), we observe:

  • Lag effects: Unemployment changes are usually slow and trend-like, while sales respond more quickly to short-term promotions and holidays.
  • Store consistency: Although sales bases differ across stores, their fluctuation patterns are highly aligned, indicating nationwide promotions and holidays are core drivers.

Strategic Recommendation Regional Operating Strategy

The distribution of average weekly sales and average unemployment by store reveals significant regional differences:

Final Conclusions and Recommendations

Based on the analysis above, we recommend:

  1. Strengthen seasonal inventory planning: Given holidays' decisive impact on sales, complete Q4 supply chain optimization 3–6 months in advance.
